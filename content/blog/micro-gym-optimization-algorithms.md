The commercial fitness industry has an unresolved yield problem. It builds large, optimises for throughput, and then discovers that the fixed overhead of a large-footprint operation destroys the margin on every incremental member.

The micro-pod model is not a fitness business. It is a real estate yield maximisation strategy applied to a category that has never been operated that way.

## The Yield Equation

Traditional gym operators calculate revenue per member. This is the wrong unit of analysis.

The correct unit is **revenue per square foot per month** — the same metric applied to every other commercial tenancy. When you run that calculation on a 5,000 sq ft commercial gym, the numbers are structurally poor. When you run it on a 350–500 sq ft automated micro-pod, the numbers are materially different.

[REAL_ESTATE_MATRIX]

These are not projections. They are the operational consequence of removing every cost centre that conventional gym models treat as fixed: staff, shared equipment contention, class scheduling infrastructure, amenity overhead.

## The Automation Variable

The yield differential is not primarily a function of size. It is a function of labour.

A staffed facility requires a minimum viable headcount regardless of utilisation rate. At 3 AM, you either pay for a person to be present or you close. Iron Oasis is designed around the premise that automated access control — biometric or PIN-gated, monitored by remote systems — eliminates this entire cost category.

The operational footprint at 3 AM is identical to the operational footprint at 6 PM. The system does not care about time-of-day.

## Site Selection Criteria

The micro-pod model is compatible with a specific set of commercial spaces that are typically undervalued in current market conditions.

**Optimal profile:**
- Ground-floor flex, retail, or light-industrial space
- 300–500 sq ft with independent HVAC access
- Dedicated electrical service (minimum 200A single-phase)
- Street-level or secure-parking adjacency
- No requirement for customer-facing frontage or signage visibility

This profile describes a category of commercial space that routinely sits vacant in secondary and tertiary markets because it is too small for conventional retail anchors and too constrained for light industrial use.

## The Deployment Architecture

Each Iron Oasis pod is a self-contained infrastructure unit. The fit-out is modular, reversible, and does not require structural modification to the host space.

Tenant improvements are limited to: access control hardware, electrical distribution, ventilation augmentation, and floor reinforcement where required. The operator — Iron Oasis or a licensed regional partner — deploys and maintains the operating system.

Revenue can be structured as a fixed lease, revenue-share, or management agreement depending on the operator's preference and the site's existing lease structure.
